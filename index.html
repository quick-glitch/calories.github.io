Calorie Counting for Weight Loss

Calorie counting for weight loss is a scientifically tested way to lose weight and get fitter. You can use a calorie calculator to determine your calorie requirements and adjust your daily calorie intake accordingly to lose weight. Here are the exact steps you can follow to set up a plan for weight loss:

  • Calculate your daily calorie requirement

You can use the calorie calculator given on this page to determine the number of calories your body burns per day on average. This is your daily calorie requirement. Eating more calories than this number will cause you weight gain, and eating less can help you lose weight.

  • Track your calorie intake

Once you have your daily calorie requirement number, start tracking your daily calorie intake for 8-10 days to get an idea of how many calories you eat per day. You can do this by using any of the multitudes of health and meal tracking applications available for smartphones.

  • Plan for a calorie deficit by counting calories

Now that you know how many calories you consume each day, you can start to cut down on your calories slowly. It is a good idea to cut down on high-calorie food items that contain too many carbs or sugars. You should also ideally cut down on processed and fried foods.

  • Prepare a healthy meal plan

Once you have cut out the high-calorie foods from your diet, you can create a new meal plan to include healthier foods like salads, lean meats, fish, etc. so that you get ample nutrition each day. However, make sure the maximum calorie deficit you aim for is 300-500 lesser calories than your daily requirement for sustainable weight loss.

  • Combine exercise with your new diet

You can combine your new diet with a routine of exercises that you can carry out each day. You can start by walking 30 minutes every day and then progressively take on more intensive exercises.

Sources of Calories

When you are reevaluating your diet choices, you need to understand the number of calories that different kinds of foods provide.

 

  • Calorie Dense Foods

Calorie dense foods are the kinds of foods that contain a very high amount of calories in foods of small quantities. For example, cheeses, desserts, sugar, nuts, and certain high sugar fruits contain a large number of calories even though they may appear to be small. A single cheese cube can contain more calories than a full chapati. You need to be careful about including these kinds of foods in your diet. It is a good idea to avoid these foods as you can end up eating a very large number of calories without actually eating too much food. However, you can occasionally consume healthy high-calorie foods like avocados or certain types of cheeses but make sure you don’t overdo it.

  • Low-Calorie Foods

Foods like vegetables and grains are great sources of nutrition while being low-calorie foods at the same time. If you want to lose weight consistently, you need to incorporate these low-calorie foods, especially vegetables into your diet. The best sources of low-calorie foods include green vegetables, carrots, egg whites, sprouts, and more. Apart from being low in calories, these foods also provide all the necessary micro and macronutrients like proteins, carbs in healthy quantities that help you remain fit and energetic.

FAQs on Calorie Calculator

  • What is the formula for calculating calories?

    A food calorie calculator provides results that are based on the basal metabolic rate or BMR. The BMR can be defined as the total energy that you expend each day at rest. BMR can be calculated using the formula 370 + 21.6 (1 - F)W. 

     W-Your weight in kilograms

    F-Percentage of your fat

  • What is the difference between kcal and calories?

    Calories indicate the specific amount of energy that food or beverages contain. The same energy is often also measured in or defined by kilocalories (kcal) or kilojoules (kJ). 1 calorie is equal to 4.18 kilojoules. Or, 1 calorie is equal to 4,184 joules.

  • What are the equations used by calorie calculators?

    An accurate calculator uses predetermined equations to help create an estimated average for the number of calories required by an individual on an average day. Here are some of the common calorie calculator equations used by a variety of calorie calculators online:

    Mifflin-St Jeor Equation

    • For men: BMR = 10W + 6.25H - 5A + 5

    • For women: BMR = 10W + 6.25H - 5A - 161

    Revised Harris-Benedict Equation

    • For men: BMR = 13.397W + 4.799H - 5.677A + 88.362

    • For women: BMR = 9.247W + 3.098H - 4.330A + 447.593

    Katch-McArdle Formula

    BMR = 370 + 21.6(1 - F)W

    In these equations,

    W refers to body weight in kilograms

    H refers to height in cms

    A refers to age

    F refers to body fat percentage
